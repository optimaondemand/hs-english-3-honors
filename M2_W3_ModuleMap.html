WEEK 3 MAP
The Government of One
For two weeks Publius has argued about how a free people should govern itself. This week the question turns inward. In 1841, in the village of Concord, Massachusetts, Ralph Waldo Emerson published an essay arguing that the harder constitution to write is the one inside a single person. "Self-Reliance" makes a demand no legislature can enforce: trust your own mind. You will read the first half of the essay this week, meet the movement called Transcendentalism, and decide for yourself whether Emerson's demand is a virtue, a danger, or both. Here is everything for Week 3, in order.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 2
How do free people order their common life, how does a free person order an inner one, and what happens when a nation denies that self-government to some of its people?
