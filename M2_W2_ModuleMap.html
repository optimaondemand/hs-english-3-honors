WEEK 2 MAP
The Machinery of Self-Government
Last week Madison diagnosed a problem sown in human nature itself. This week two harder questions follow from it: if people cannot be fully trusted with power, how do you build a government that can be trusted with it anyway? And can a branch with no army and no treasury still pose a threat? Federalist No. 51 and No. 78 make the founders' case; read closely enough to test it yourself. You also pick up the research tool you will use for the rest of the year, JStor. Here is everything for Week 2, in order.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 2
How do free people order their common life, how does a free person order an inner one, and what happens when a nation denies that self-government to some of its people?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 2.05: Federalist 51 — If Men Were Angels (literature 1 of 3)
Read the first half of No. 51 and meet Madison's hardest premise: government exists because people are not angels.
2
Lesson 2.06: Federalist 51 — Checks and Balances (literature 2 of 3)
Finish No. 51 and trace the machinery Madison builds to keep power in check with power.
3
Lesson 2.07: Federalist 78 — The Least Dangerous Branch (literature 3 of 3)
Hamilton takes on the courts, the branch the ratification debate trusted least. Judge for yourself whether his case holds.
4
Recitation Checkpoint: Patrick Henry, "Give Me Liberty" (ungraded checkpoint)
Choose your passage from Henry's 1775 speech and read it aloud once. Details are in Lesson 2.07. This rehearses the skills for the graded Douglass recitation in Week 7.
5
Lesson 2.08: Grammar & Vocabulary (grammar + vocabulary)
Conditional sentences, the "if… then" machinery of No. 51, plus Word Study from this week's papers.
6
Research Toolkit: JStor (lesson)
Your full walkthrough: search, filter, judge, synthesize, and cite scholarly sources. You need this before Feeder 4.
7
Discussion: Should Government Distrust Us? (graded, post + one reply)
Apply Madison's frank view of human nature to one present-day institution.
8
Feeder 4: Two Sources, One Synthesis (graded, Word doc)
Pull two credible JStor sources into one synthesis paragraph with MLA citations, research muscle for Publius Returns.
