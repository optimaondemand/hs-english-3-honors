WEEK 6 MAP
The Final Act
This week you read the final act of Hamlet and watch every thread the play has been carrying, the ghost's demand, the delay, the schemes at court, come to a head. You also learn to cite the play correctly for your essay and project. Read each scene before you look ahead; this map tells you what to watch for, not what happens. Here is everything for Week 6, in order.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 1
What does it mean to know yourself, and what does conscience demand when knowledge calls for action?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 6.01: The Graveyard (literature 1 of 3)
Read Act V, scene 1. Watch for why Shakespeare opens his most serious act with jokes, and what a skull can teach a prince.
2
Lesson 6.02: The Readiness Is All (literature 2 of 3)
Act V, scene 2, first half. Watch how Hamlet's stance toward death has changed since "To be, or not to be," and weigh what his sea voyage reveals about him.
3
Lesson 6.03: The Rest Is Silence (literature 3 of 3)
Read the final scene first; the lesson teaches you the shape of a tragic catastrophe, then sends you to read before any discussion.
4
Lesson 6.04: Citing the Play (grammar + vocabulary)
MLA citation by act, scene, and line; quoting verse. You will need this for the project.
5
Discussion: A Just Ending? (graded, post + one reply)
Has Hamlet changed by Act V, and is the ending just or merely final?
