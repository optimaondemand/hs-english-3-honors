WEEK 7 MAP
The Self Reclaimed
Two weeks ago you met the self denied. Last week you watched literacy pry the system open. This week the Narrative finishes its arc. Douglass stands up to the man hired to break him, and marks the moment with the most famous sentence in the book: "You have seen how a man was made a slave; you shall see how a slave was made a man" (Narrative, ch. 10). Then he escapes, refuses to tell you how, and ends the book at the exact moment he begins his life's work: speaking. Finally, in the Appendix, he turns and addresses the churches of the republic directly, holding them to their own creed. Read chapters 8 and 9 quickly as a bridge (the valuation of the property, the return to St. Michael's), then slow down for chapters 10 and 11 and the Appendix. And this is recording week: the graded Douglass recitation is due. Here is everything for Week 7, in order.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 2
How do free people order their common life, how does a free person order an inner one, and what happens when a nation denies that self-government to some of its people?
This week Douglass answers the third clause in his own person: the denied self takes itself back, and then demands that the nation live up to its words.
