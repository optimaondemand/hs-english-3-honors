WEEK 8 MAP
Write Your Lost Scene
This week you become the playwright. Using the plan from Lesson 7.02 and your Week 4 Character Dossier, you draft an original scene or soliloquy for your chosen character. Two short craft lessons, then you write.
🎯 THE MODULE 1 PROJECT
The Elsinore Stage: an original missing scene or soliloquy for a secondary character in Hamlet, in loose blank verse, that could plausibly belong in the real play. You draft it this week (Week 8) and finalize it next week (Week 9).
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 8.01: Drafting in a Character's Voice (project craft)
Turn your plan into lines: opening, turn, and close, in blank verse.
2
Lesson 8.02: Sounding Like the Play (project craft)
Match register and diction; avoid anachronism; stay consistent with the real plot.
3
The Elsinore Stage Workshop (ungraded scaffolding tool)
Reflect, brainstorm, organize, and stylize your opening lines; check your rhythm with Claude and log it; download the PDF.
4
Discussion: The Elsinore Stage Elevator Pitch (graded, post by mid-week, reply by end of week)
Pitch your character and scene, then give real feedback to at least two classmates before you finish drafting.
5
The Elsinore Stage: Draft (graded, completion)
Submit your full draft scene. Next week you revise it into the final.
✅ BEFORE YOU CLOSE WEEK 8, CONFIRM

I submitted a complete draft (not an outline) of my lost scene.
☐

☐
