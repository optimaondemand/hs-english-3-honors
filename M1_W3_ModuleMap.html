Anchor Text
Hamlet, Act III, sc. 1–2
WEEK 3 MAP
The Mirror and the Mousetrap
This week Hamlet stops circling and builds a test of his own. You read the play's most famous soliloquy, hear Hamlet tell the actors what theater is for, and watch him set that test in motion. Read closely enough to answer it yourself: does the trap work? Here is everything for Week 3 and the order to do it in.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 1
What does it mean to know yourself, and what does conscience demand when knowledge calls for action?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 3.01: The Question and the Watchers (literature 1 of 3)
Read Act III, scene 1: "To be, or not to be" and the nunnery scene, with two listeners hidden behind it.
2
Lesson 3.02: The Mirror Up to Nature (literature 2 of 3)
Begin Act III, scene 2: Hamlet coaches the actors and tells them what a play is for.
3
Lesson 3.03: The Mousetrap (literature 3 of 3)
Finish Act III, scene 2: watch how Hamlet's staged play tests the king, and mark the moment where the scene turns.
4
Lesson 3.04: Sentence Craft & Word Study (grammar + vocabulary)
Balance your sentences with parallel structure; add two Act III words to your Journal.
5
Discussion: What Does the Mousetrap Prove? (graded, post + one reply)
Make one specific claim about what Claudius's reaction proves, with lines from 3.2, and answer a classmate.
6
Recitation Rehearsal Checkpoint (ungraded, nothing to turn in)
Read the soliloquy you chose in Week 2 aloud, start to finish, at least twice this week. Details in Lesson 3.01.
