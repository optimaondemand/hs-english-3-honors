Anchor Text
Hamlet, Act II
WEEK 2 MAP
Watchers and Players
Two months have passed inside the play, and everyone at Elsinore is watching everyone else. This week you read all of Act II: watchers set on nearly everyone, Hamlet's language changing shape, and a troupe of actors whose arrival changes everything. Watch for what Hamlet decides to do with them. Here is everything for Week 2 and the order to do it in.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 1
What does it mean to know yourself, and what does conscience demand when knowledge calls for action?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 2.01: Spying at Elsinore (literature 1 of 3)
Read Act II, scene 1; study the surveillance motif and how the play uses reported, offstage action.
2
Lesson 2.02: Wit and Prose (literature 2 of 3)
Read the first half of Act II, scene 2; hear Hamlet drop out of verse into prose and learn why the shift matters.
3
Lesson 2.03: Rogue and Peasant Slave (literature 3 of 3)
Finish Act II: the Players arrive, and Hamlet turns his wit on himself in the play's second great soliloquy. Watch for what he decides to do next.
4
Lesson 2.04: Quoting the Play & Word Study (grammar + vocabulary)
Learn to weave a quoted phrase smoothly into your own sentence, with the punctuation right; add two Act II words to your Journal.
5
Discussion: The Antic Disposition (graded, post + one reply)
Is Hamlet's madness real, performed, or both? Stake a claim on specific Act II lines and answer a classmate.
6
Choose your recitation soliloquy (ungraded, note it in your Journal)
This week the recitation thread begins. Pick the Hamlet soliloquy you will memorize and perform for the graded recitation in Week 7. Details in Lesson 2.03.
🔄 RUNNING ALL MODULE (keep going, do not turn in yet)
Reading Journal (Module 1 Organizer): add your Act II notes and this week's Word Study words to the same Word document; a Reading Journal Check comes later in the module.
Recitation thread: once you choose your soliloquy this week, start reading it aloud a few times each week. Memory grows by small repeated visits, not one long cram. The graded performance is in Week 7.
