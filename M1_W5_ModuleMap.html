Hamlet, Act IV
WEEK 5 MAP
The Kingdom Comes Apart
Polonius is dead, and Act IV is the fallout. Hamlet is shipped toward England and delivers his last soliloquy; grief reaches Ophelia and Laertes in two very different forms; and the king turns what he is handed to his own advantage. Watch what each person in the court does with grief this week, and what it costs them. This is also the week of your graded Reading Journal Check. Here is everything for Week 5 and the order to do it in.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 1
What does it mean to know yourself, and what does conscience demand when knowledge calls for action?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 5.01: How All Occasions (literature 1 of 3)
Read Act IV, scenes 1–4: the fallout of the killing, the road to England, and Hamlet's last soliloquy on a plain in Denmark.
2
Lesson 5.02: Ophelia's Madness (literature 2 of 3)
Read Act IV, scene 5: what grief does to Ophelia in the open court, and what it drives her brother home to demand.
3
Lesson 5.03: The Trap Is Set (literature 3 of 3)
Finish Act IV, scenes 6–7: Claudius turns Laertes's grief into a plot, and the queen closes the act with news from offstage.
4
Lesson 5.04: Semicolons, Colons & Word Study (grammar + vocabulary)
Join and introduce ideas with the semicolon and the colon; add two Act IV words to your Journal.
5
Discussion: The Sons Who Act (graded, post + one reply)
Laertes and Fortinbras both act where Hamlet hesitates. Pick one and argue what his example reveals, with Act IV lines.
6
Reading Journal Check (graded, 10 pts, Word doc)
The checkpoint you have been building toward since Week 1: upload your Module 1 Organizer as it stands, Weeks 1–5.

Recitation Rehearsal: Begin Memorizing (ungraded, nothing to turn in)
