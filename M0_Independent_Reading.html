YOUR READING LIFE
Read one book you choose, each quarter, for the pleasure of it
Alongside the works we study together, you keep a reading life of your own. Each quarter you pick one book from the menu below and read it at your own pace. This is meant to be enjoyable, not another heavy assignment, so the way you are held accountable is deliberately light.
📚 WHY THIS IS PART OF THE COURSE

The strongest readers are people who read on their own, by choice, for years. This part of the course exists to build that habit while you still have a class supporting it. You choose the book, so it is yours; the point is to finish a quarter having genuinely read and enjoyed something, not to be tested on it.

🧭 HOW THE QUARTER WORKS (THREE LIGHT STEPS)
1
Choose a book (Week 1).
Pick one title from the menu below, or propose your own for quick teacher approval. Do not pick a book we are studying together that quarter.
2
Keep a one-line reading log (as you go).
In your Module Organizer, add a quick line whenever you read: the date, the pages or minutes, and one sentence of reaction. That is the whole log. It builds the habit and takes seconds.
3
Share one short response (the Quarterly Reading Check).
Near the end of the quarter, do one of the responses below. It is graded for completion, not graded like an essay. Or, if you would rather talk than write, sign up for a three-minute conversation with your facilitator instead.
🎭 CHOOSE ONE RESPONSE (WHATEVER SUITS THE BOOK AND YOU)
🎤 Book talk
A two-to-three minute recording: what it is, one thing you loved, and who should read it.
✉️ Recommendation letter
A short letter to a friend making the case for (or against) the book.
🔖 Favorite passage
Copy one passage you love and add a few sentences on why it stayed with you.
🔗 Connection
One page linking your book to the work we studied this quarter (a shared theme, a mirrored choice).
💬 Prefer talking? Swap the written response for a three-minute facilitator conference. Live students can do this in Teams; On-Demand students by appointment. Same credit either way.
Reading log · example
Sep 9 · 20 min · Hooked already; the narrator is funnier than I expected.
Sep 12 · 25 pp · Slower here, but I want to know what she decides.
Sep 16 · 30 min · That twist changes how I read the first chapter.
