Week 4
Anchor Text
Hamlet, Act III, sc. 3–4
WEEK 4 MAP
The Prayer and the Point of No Return
Last week the Mousetrap gave Hamlet his certainty. This week, ask what a person does with certainty once he has it, in a moment when he chooses to act and in a moment when he does not stop to choose at all. You finish Act III, watch how the Ghost's return complicates what counts as real, and begin scouting the character you will write for in the module project. Here is everything for Week 4 and the order to do it in.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 1
What does it mean to know yourself, and what does conscience demand when knowledge calls for action?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 4.01: The Prayer Scene (literature 1 of 3)
Read Act III, scene 3: Claudius kneels to pray, and Hamlet stands over him with a sword. Watch what Hamlet decides, and watch closely whose account of the prayer you are given by the scene's end.
2
Lesson 4.02: The Closet Scene (literature 2 of 3)
Begin Act III, scene 4: Hamlet confronts his mother in her private chamber. Watch what happens when a voice cries out from behind the arras, and ask what one instant, unplanned choice can change for good.
3
Lesson 4.03: The Ghost Returns (literature 3 of 3)
Finish Act III, scene 4: a familiar figure returns to the room. Watch closely who reacts to it and who does not, then weigh what that gap can tell you. Real visitor or projection? The text leaves it open, and so will we.
4
Lesson 4.04: Sentence Craft & Word Study (grammar + vocabulary)
Add information cleanly with appositives and other nonrestrictive elements, set off with commas; add two Act III words to your Journal.
5
Discussion: Conviction or Excuse? (graded, post + one reply)
Hamlet spares Claudius at prayer and gives a reason. Argue whether the reason is sincere, with lines from 3.3, and answer a classmate.
6
Feeder 2: Character Dossier & Motivation Brief (graded, 15 pts, Word doc)
Pick a secondary figure and write a short expository brief on what they want, and the gap between their public words and their private aim. This scouts the character for your Module 1 project, The Elsinore Stage.
7
Recitation Rehearsal Checkpoint (ungraded, nothing to turn in)
Rehearse your soliloquy aloud again this week. This is the final week to change your choice; after this week you commit to it. Details in Lesson 4.03.
🔄 RUNNING ALL MODULE (keep going, do not turn in yet)
Reading Journal (Module 1 Organizer): add your notes on Act III, scenes 3 and 4, and this week's Word Study words; a Reading Journal Check comes later in the module.
Recitation thread: rehearse aloud again this week, and settle your choice for good. If you have been wavering between two soliloquies, this is the week to decide; from Week 5 on you memorize the one you named. The graded recitation is Week 7.
