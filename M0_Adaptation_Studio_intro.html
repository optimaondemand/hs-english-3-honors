THE HONORS PROJECT
The Adaptation Studio
This year you are a director. Alongside everything in the regular course, you will spend the full year adapting one unit of this syllabus into an immersive virtual-reality work, and defending the choices you make as an argument about that unit's central question. This page is the overview; each quarter's milestones will give you the specific steps.
🎬 CHOOSE YOUR MODE
Dramatic adaptation (a VR film)
Stage and capture a pivotal scene, or a compressed reimagining, so your directorial choices render the unit's question in a space a viewer can stand inside.
Documentary (a VR experience)
Build a researched, narrated walkthrough of the unit's world, organized around a thesis you argue and defend from real scholarship.

Either way, you pose a genuine question the text leaves open, take a position, and narrate in your own voice. That narration also counts toward the year's recitation thread.

🎥 THE YEAR AS A PRODUCTION PIPELINE
QUARTER 1 · HAMLET
Pre-production I
Learn film grammar; lock your unit and mode; first research; write a treatment.
QUARTER 2 · FOUNDERS
Pre-production II
Script and narration; storyboard; first accuracy review; full design package.
QUARTER 3 · JANE EYRE
Production
Build the spaces; capture your work and narration; rough cut and critique.
QUARTER 4 · F451
Post & Premiere
Final cut and captions; final accuracy review; publish; premiere and speak it live.
You work in small biweekly milestones, each a graded checkpoint that feeds the next. The grade rewards the whole pipeline, not a single submission at the end.
📦 WHAT YOU DELIVER BY YEAR'S END
▶ The finished VR work, captioned so anyone can review it.
▶ A director's statement defending your interpretation from scholarship.
▶ An annotated JStor bibliography.
▶ An accuracy statement naming your sources and any dramatic license you took.
▶ A disclosed AI-use log.
▶ A live delivery of your narration or a key passage at the year-end symposium (this is your Quarter 4 elocution capstone).
🎯 TWO THINGS THAT MATTER FROM DAY ONE

Accuracy is built in, not assumed. You keep a Source and Line Log all year: a simple record of the exact evidence behind your choices, with every line you draw on logged to its act, scene, and line, and every fact logged to its source. It works like the citations behind an essay. Two accuracy reviews check your work against the actual sources, never against memory.

You build solo, in a space you choose. The VR work is captured by whatever recording method your platform supports, and captions are required so it can be graded outside a headset. The specific how-to steps will be provided with each milestone.

🤖 USING CLAUDE ON THIS PROJECT

You will use Claude as a working assistant on the Studio, and the specific steps live inside each milestone (they are not a separate add-on). Claude helps you brainstorm structure, check a source, or flag a possible anachronism. It never does your research, your directorial judgment, or your narration. Each milestone that uses Claude includes a short log of what you asked, what it gave you, what you kept, and what was wrong.

If you have not yet set Claude up, do the two Working with Claude pages in Start Here first. They cover getting Claude Code running, prompting well, keeping records, managing tokens, verifying outputs, and saving your work to OneDrive.
