WORKING WITH CLAUDE · PAGE 1
Getting Claude Code Running for This Course
Nothing here is graded. Read it once now, then come back to it whenever you set up a project.
🎯 THE ONE RULE, BEFORE ANYTHING ELSE

Claude is an assistant, not an author. In this course you use it to help your thinking: to check a fact, sort your notes, or talk through a plan. You do not use it to do your reading, your interpreting, your drafting, or your word choices. Those are the actual work of an English student, and they are what your grade rewards. Every place a project invites you to use Claude, it also asks you to log what you did, so the line between your work and its help stays clear.

🤖 WHAT CLAUDE CODE IS

Claude is an AI assistant you talk to in plain language. Claude Code is a version of it that runs on a computer and can open, read, and write the actual files in a folder you point it at. That matters for this course, because your work lives in real files: a Word draft, a PDF you downloaded, an outline, a set of notes. Claude Code can look at those files with you and help you work on them, instead of you copying and pasting text back and forth.

You sign in with your own account. Your conversations and files are yours; do not use anyone else's login, and do not share yours.

👀 WHAT IT CAN AND CANNOT DO FOR YOU
✅ GOOD USES (IT HELPS)
▸ Explain a hard sentence or an unfamiliar word.
▸ Check a date or a historical fact you then verify.
▸ Sort or reorganize notes you already wrote.
▸ Ask "what am I missing?" about your own outline.
▸ Turn your rough bullet points into a cleaner list to react to.
⛔ NOT FOR THIS COURSE (IT REPLACES YOU)
▸ Writing your essay, scene, or discussion post.
▸ Reading the text so you do not have to.
▸ Producing your interpretation or your thesis.
▸ Choosing your words, images, or phrasing.
▸ Giving you "quotes" you paste in without checking.
A good test: if the tool did the thinking, it was the wrong use. If it helped you do your thinking, it was the right one.
🔧 GET IT RUNNING (FOUR STEPS)

Exact setup steps depend on your device, so follow the current official setup guide and ask your facilitator if a step does not match your screen. The shape is always the same:

1
Sign in with your own account. Use the account your school set up for you. Keep the password somewhere safe.
2
Install Claude Code and open it. It runs in a simple text window (a terminal). You type what you want in plain language and press enter; there is no complicated menu to learn.
3
Point it at your course folder. Open it inside your Optima → ELA → ELA11 folder in OneDrive so it can see the files you are working on. Now it can read your draft or your notes when you ask.
4
Try a first, safe request. Ask something low-stakes, such as: "List the files in this folder," or "Explain what a soliloquy is in two sentences." You are just confirming it works and getting used to talking to it.
🔗 Official setup guide: [Teacher: link the current Claude Code setup guide and your school's account instructions here.] Stuck on a step? Email your facilitator.
📄 THE FILE TYPES YOU WILL MEET

Different work comes in different file types. Claude Code can read all of these; knowing what each is keeps you organized.

.docx
Microsoft Word. Your drafts, essays, and the Reading Journal.
.pdf
A fixed page format. Readings, worksheets you download, and the PDF you submit for some projects.
.txt / .md
Plain text and Markdown. Quick notes and outlines with no formatting to fuss over.
.png / .jpg
Images. Storyboards, a photo of handwritten work, a film still you are analyzing.
