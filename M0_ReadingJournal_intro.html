YOUR READING JOURNAL
One document, kept all quarter
Your Reading Journal is where your thinking lives while you read. It is a single Word document, one per module, that you add to across the whole quarter. It is not busywork; it is the raw material your discussions, feeders, and quarter project are built from.
❗ THE THREE THINGS TO KNOW
1
What it is. One Word document per module, called the Module Organizer. You open it once and keep adding to it each week. You do not turn it in every week.
2
How and when you submit it. Partway through each module there is a graded Reading Journal Check. When you reach it, you upload the same document through Canvas using the OneDrive tab. Your teacher reads it for completeness and effort, not for perfect answers.
3
Where it lives. In your own OneDrive, at Optima → ELA → ELA11. It stays yours; you can reopen and reread it any time, including when you study for the project.

💭 Your evolving claim
One claim about the text that grows as you read. You add a dated line each time your thinking shifts.
💭 ABOUT THE EVOLVING CLAIM

This is the one part students ask about most, so here is exactly what it is. Your evolving claim is your current best answer to the module's question, written as one sentence and updated over time. It is not a plot summary and not a new topic each week. It is a single line of thinking that you watch grow.

Each time your reading shifts, add a dated line with two parts: your claim right now, and the line or scene that changed your mind. Keep the earlier versions; do not erase them. The value is in seeing your interpretation move.

EXAMPLE (yours will differ)
Wk 1: Hamlet is a grieving son who sees through a corrupt court. What struck me: "I have that within which passeth show" (1.2).
Wk 4: I am less sure he is only a victim; "To be, or not to be" (3.1) reads to me as delay dressed up as philosophy.
Wk 8: I think he is admirable and self-defeating at once, and the way he treats Rosencrantz and Guildenstern makes his conscience harder to defend.
