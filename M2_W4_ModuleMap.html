WEEK 4 MAP
Two Self-Governments Meet
This week you finish "Self-Reliance," then do something new: you put the module's two voices in the same room. Madison built self-government for a nation; Emerson demands it of a soul. Where do they back each other up, and where do they collide? By the end of the week you will have written your first graded synthesis of the quarter, and turned in your Reading Journal at the mid-module check. Here is everything for Week 4, in order.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 2
How do free people order their common life, how does a free person order an inner one, and what happens when a nation denies that self-government to some of its people?
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 2.13: A Foolish Consistency (literature 1 of 3)
The hobgoblin sentence, read whole: why Emerson prizes changing your mind, and what "To be great is to be misunderstood" does and does not claim.
2
Lesson 2.14: The Self and the Joint-Stock Company (literature 2 of 3)
Emerson's harshest metaphor for society, and the real costs of nonconformity he admits in the same breath.
3
Lesson 2.15: The Bridge: Madison Meets Emerson (literature 3 of 3)
State self-government beside soul self-government: where the two arguments support each other and where they collide.
4
Lesson 2.16: Grammar & Vocabulary (grammar + vocabulary)
The balanced sentence, the form comparison was made for, plus Word Study from "Self-Reliance."
5
Discussion: Can Emerson's Individualist Live in Madison's Republic? (graded, post + one reply)
Take a position on whether the two visions can coexist, anchored in both texts.
6
Synthesis Quick-Write: Madison Meets Emerson (graded, Word doc)
One paragraph that puts the two writers in conversation on self-government, with a concession-and-counter move and citations to both.
7
Reading Journal Check (Mid-Module) (graded, completion; due this week)
Upload your Module 2 Organizer as it stands: Weeks 1 through 4 notes, Word Study, and your appeals watch. This is the scheduled checkpoint announced in Week 1.
