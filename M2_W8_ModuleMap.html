WEEK 8 MAP · MODULE CLOSE
Enter the Conversation, Then Finish It
The reading is done. Publius built the republic on paper, Emerson told you to trust your own mind, and Douglass showed you what it costs to seize a self back from a nation that denied it. This week you stop listening and start writing, and you finish what you start. Two short craft lessons get your draft moving, a mid-week checkpoint gets it on the page, a revision lesson makes it stronger, and by the end of the week you submit the finished op-ed under your own pseudonym. This is the last shared week of Module 2.
🎯 THE QUESTION BEHIND MODULE 2
How do free people order their common life, how does a free person order an inner one, and what happens when a nation denies that self-government to some of its people?
📰 THE MODULE 2 PROJECT
Publius Returns: an argumentative op-ed to your fellow citizens, written under a pseudonym as Publius wrote, taking a clear position on a live civic question that this module's three texts argue from different angles, and supported by a researched synthesis of credible JStor sources. You draft it, revise it, and finalize it, all within this one week.
📋 DO THIS, IN THIS ORDER
1
Lesson 2.29: A Conversation Across Texts (project craft, 1 of 3)
Put Publius, Emerson, and Douglass around one table, choose your live civic question, and map what each voice gives your argument.
2
Lesson 2.30: Writing as Publius (project craft, 2 of 3)
The op-ed as a form: a pseudonymous public voice, the three appeals, the concession-and-counter move, and your JStor research woven in with MLA citations.
3
Discussion: Publius Returns Elevator Pitch (graded, post by mid-week, reply by end of week)
Pitch your civic question and claim, then challenge at least two classmates' arguments before you finish drafting.
4
Publius Returns: Draft (graded, completion, Word doc, mid-week)
Submit your full draft op-ed, roughly 500 to 700 words under your pseudonym. You revise it later this week into the final.
5
Lesson 2.31: The Revision Workshop (project craft, 3 of 3)
Three passes, big to small: the argument, the sentence, the proof. Turn your draft into the version you sign and submit.
6
Publius Returns: Final (graded project, Word doc, end of week)
Your revised op-ed to the citizenry, teacher-graded. This is the module's main assessment — includes publishing it as a simple website with Claude's help.
7
Civic Address (optional): Take the Podium (completion, recorded)
Record a 2 to 3 minute excerpt of your op-ed, delivered as a speech to a civic forum.
8
Module 2 Closing Reflection (reflection)
Answer the module question again and reflect on what changed since Week 1.
🔄 RUNNING ALL MODULE (keep going)
Reading Journal (Module 2 Organizer): your Journal does real work this week. The question you choose, the three-voice map from Lesson 2.29, and the op-ed skeleton from Lesson 2.30 all live there before they become your draft, and your revision log from Lesson 2.31 lives there too. Keep citing as you have all module: (Federalist 51), ("Self-Reliance"), (Narrative, ch. 7).
